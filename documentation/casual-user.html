Casual User Persona

Name : Charlie Charles

Technology

  • Dell Inspiron 27, all in one Desktop computer, Windows 11
  • Iphone 13, IOS 17
  • Apple Watch Series 5, WatchOS 10

Technical Proficiencies

  • Charlie isn't the most tech-savy person.
  • Uses google regularly to help him in his daily life.
  • Gets frustrated by his searches not being optimized and giving him the answer that he is looking for.

Attitudes

  • He feels like websites should be east to digest and should look professional.
  • Wants to have resources available to help with his indoor plants.
  • Is new to taking care of plants and doesn't know where to start.

Needs

  • Needs to know how to take care of his brand new Aloe Vera plant.
  • Needs to know which plants are similar to his Aloe Vera because he loves the way it looks and wants another one.
  • Needs to know this information quickly and to be displayed in a simple manner.

User Story :

As a casual user I want to search for ways to take care of my plant and to find similar plants.

Use Case :

Charlie just bought an aloe vera plant from the farmers market. He loves it. When he gets home he hops online to search, "How to care for an Aloe Vera plant." Our website pops up. He clicks on the URL.

Interaction flow :

  1. Charlie enters the website.
  2. He is presented with an option of logging in or continuing as a guest.
  3. He clicks on the guest option.
  4. That takes him to another screen that presents the options; Looking for a new plant? or, Plant Care.
  5. He clicks on Plant Care.
  6. That takes him to a new screen with a question box asking for the species of plant.
  7. He types in, "aloe vera", and hits enter.
  8. The screen loads with info cards with different types of Aloe Vera.
  9. He clicks on the card with a picture that looks just like his plant.
  10. The page loads and shows him a summary of basic needs for the plant and what level of care it needs.

PreCondition: Charlie enters our website.

PostCondition: Charlie knows how to take care of his new plant.
